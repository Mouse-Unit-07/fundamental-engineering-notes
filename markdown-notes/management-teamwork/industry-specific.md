## Overview

- ...There are a bunch of roles that all sound the same
- The point (regardless of what the role is called) is that there's a common role w/ the associated management/leadership responsibilities across different companies

## Engineering Director

- Refers to a role where you're managing multiple teams
- Responsible for organization's overall technical competence- guiding and growing the competence in the whole team is necessary w/ training and hiring
- Strong technical background
- Should be spending time researching new technologies and staying up to date w/ trends in the tech industry
- Expected to help debug and triage critical systems, and should understand systems well enough to run code reviews and help research problems as needed
- Concerned w/ continuously evaluating/refining development/infrastructure standards and processes
- Responsible for creating and growing next generation leadership and management talent in the organization

## Engineering Lead

- Refers to a role dedicated to finding bottlenecks in systems
- Characteristics include:
  - Spending less time writing code (but involved w/ small deliverables, bug fixes, features, etc)
  - Finds roadblocks/bottlenecks to guide a team to success
  - Responsible for identifying high-value projects to prioritize them
  - Identifies areas of technical debt and performs cost/benefit analysis for resolving the debt and generates suggested timelines for integrating the resolution into work being done
- Technical understanding and knowledge of the work being done is essential to understand what's being fought, and to propose suggestions to improve

## Tech Lead

- Refers to a technical leadership role (not necessarily people management, but rather project management)
- Reflects competency for communication, internal networking, engineering, and time management / task prioritization
- Balancing becomes the core challenge
- Includes system architects, business analysts, project planners, software team leads, etc
- Bad tech lead characteristics:
  - Hates talking to people
  - Distracted by technical details all the time
  - Micromanager
- Good tech lead characteristics
  - Focus on the whole team's productivity
  - Empowered to make independent decisions for the team
  - Learns how to handle difficult management/leadership situations
  - Still does engineering work (~30% of the time w/ the team)
  - Willing to step away from code (specific engineering work) to figure out how to balance technical commitments w/ the work the whole team needs
